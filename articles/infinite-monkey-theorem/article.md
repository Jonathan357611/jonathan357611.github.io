# Infinite Monkey Theorem

This project of mine demonstrates the so called "Infinite typing monkey theorem".

[Here](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cEbX8EM3IEU) is good explanation for it.

Basically it says that if you let a monkey (In our case the much faster computer) just type random letters for an
infinite period of time, it will eventually come up with **any** possible thing that can be written.
Including every book that was ever written, your super secret messages or even your diary :)
Of course you shouldn't forget that these examples take indeed a very long time.
As a matter of fact: A very very unimaginable period of time.

This does limit this whole thing a bit but at least we can apply this to small words.


![Image](https://cdn.discordapp.com/attachments/1012390715478777956/1045875520551800902/image.png)

Here is a small demonstration using the word "demonstration".
As you can see my okay-ish computer can do around 20k leters/second.
It also shows the chance for the word to occur per try and the expected time left, which is... well, very long,
6486907.7 years to be exact, for a medium-sized word.

A sentence with 45 letters takes my computer 1.3208366050807478e+60 seconds which is 41854786320000000000000000000000000000000000000000000 years if I did everything right which is 30329555304347826000000000000000000000000000 times older then our universe.


For every letter you have to take the chance to the power of 27 (letters in alphabet + whitespace)
